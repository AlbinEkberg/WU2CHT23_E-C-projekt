Arcitecture through time

A brief history

From humble caves to monumental pyramids, the history of buildings traces humanity's evolution. Early settlements embraced mud-brick and stone, while ancient civilizations like the Greeks and Romans introduced enduring architectural elements. The Middle Ages saw the rise of Gothic cathedrals, and the Renaissance revived classical influences. The Industrial Revolution ushered in structural innovations, paving the way for the soaring heights of modern skyscrapers, marking an ongoing saga of human ingenuity in construction.
